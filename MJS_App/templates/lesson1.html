Lesson #1

How to Build Chords (with extensions!)

This is a chord symbol. Musicians should know how to read these symbols in addition to standard clef notation.

Chord symbols can be broken down into 2 parts: the root and extensions/adjustments. Let's first talk about building a triad from the root note.

Recall the major scale associated with the root of the chord (i.e. scale degree 1 = the root).

Find scale degrees 1, 3, & 5. *Just about all chords are built on intervals (distance between notes) that are a third apart: C to E, D to F, E to G, F to A, etc.

Congratulations! You have just built your first chord: a "C Major chord". This chord can be played in any octave (or inversion) and still keep the same name. But what about the extension?

Extensions are where things get interesting. Musicians (especially those who play jazz) have come up with shorthands for extensions. These shorthands have come to represent a certain "sound" that a composer/musician wants to produce. Above is the chord symbol for a C Major 7 chord. Take note of the spelling: what scale degree was added?
